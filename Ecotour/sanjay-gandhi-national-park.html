Sanjay Gandhi National Park

Maharashtra, India | National Park | Best Time: October to March | Monsoon (June to September)

About the Park

The Sanjay Gandhi National Park (SGNP), located in Mumbai, India, is a vast green space offering a wide variety of flora and fauna. With trekking trails, serene lakes, and a rich cultural heritage, it’s a perfect escape from the hustle of city life. It’s one of the most visited parks in Asia, known for its biodiversity and peaceful environment.

  • Morning: Begin your adventure at the Kanheri Caves, a complex of ancient Buddhist rock-cut caves that offer insights into history and spirituality. Admire the intricate carvings, large prayer halls, and ancient inscriptions that date back to the 1st century BCE. Spend 2-3 hours exploring these caves and soaking in the serene atmosphere amidst the dense greenery.

  • Afternoon: Take a break for lunch at one of the park's designated picnic spots or nearby cafes. Post-lunch, embark on a nature trail, where you can enjoy a leisurely walk through the forest. Look out for exotic birds and spot small mammals like monkeys and deer. The park’s calm ambiance and fresh air make for a rejuvenating experience.

  • Evening: Relax by Gandhi Tekdi, a peaceful viewpoint within the park offering panoramic views of the lush greenery. End your day by the lake area, where you can sit back, enjoy the tranquil surroundings, and watch the sunset.
  • Morning: Start your day with the Lion and Tiger Safari, a guided tour in a secure vehicle that brings you up close to these majestic animals. This experience is thrilling and safe, perfect for visitors of all ages. After the safari, visit the Mini Zoo, which houses various species of reptiles, birds, and small mammals.

  • Midday: Take a break for lunch at the park’s restaurant or carry packed food. Enjoy your meal amidst the serene surroundings.

  • Afternoon: Head to the Dianthubhakti Waterfall (seasonal), a picturesque spot ideal for photography and relaxation. The waterfall is especially stunning during or right after the monsoon season. Engage in a short trek near the waterfall area for a closer connection with nature.

  • Evening: Conclude your visit with a peaceful stroll along one of the park’s lesser-explored trails or simply unwind in the open picnic areas. Depart the park, carrying with you the memories of a perfect blend of adventure, history, and nature.
